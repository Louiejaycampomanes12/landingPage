Mountain Province is a landlocked province located in the Cordillera Administrative Region of Luzon, Philippines. Its capital is Bontoc. Here are some key demographic facts about Mountain Province


  • According to the Philippine Statistics Authority, the province had a total population of 140,439 in the 2000 Census of Population and Housing. The population has been growing at an annual rate of 1.88 percent from 1990 to 2000 and 1.54 percent from 1995 to 2000.


  • In 2015, the population of Mountain Province was recorded at 161,540, with a male population of 83,370 (51.6%) and a female population of 78,170 (48.4%). The province has experienced a population increase of 236.9% from 1975 to 2015 and a 26% increase from 2000 to 2015.


  • The median age in Mountain Province is 22.2 years, with the male and female median ages both around 22.2 to 22.3 years.

  • Mountain Province covers an area of 1,974.2 square kilometers.

  • Please note that these statistics may have changed over time
