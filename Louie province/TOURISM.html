Mountain Province, located in the Cordillera Administrative Region of the Philippines, is known for its stunning landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and unique attractions. Here are some aspects related to tourism in Mountain Province:


  • Banaue Rice Terraces: One of the main draws of Mountain Province is the world-famous Banaue Rice Terraces. Carved into the mountains over 2,000 years ago by the Ifugao people, these terraces are a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Visitors come to witness the breathtaking beauty of these intricately designed terraces


  • Cultural Heritage: The province is home to various indigenous communities, including the Ifugao and Kankanaey people. Tourists can immerse themselves in the local culture, experiencing traditional dances, rituals, and enjoying the warm hospitality of the locals.


  • Sagada: This town in Mountain Province is known for its mystical and picturesque landscapes. Popular attractions in Sagada include the hanging coffins, Sumaguing Cave, and Bomod-Ok Falls. Sagada is also a hub for adventure activities like trekking and caving.

  • Mount Pulag: As one of the highest peaks in Luzon, Mount Pulag is a favorite among trekkers and nature enthusiasts. The mountain is renowned for its sea of clouds, providing a surreal and mesmerizing sunrise experience.


  • Local Handicrafts and Souvenirs: Visitors have the opportunity to purchase traditional handicrafts, woven items, and other souvenirs crafted by the indigenous communities. These items often showcase the rich cultural heritage of the region.


  • Trekking and Hiking: The mountainous terrain offers numerous trekking and hiking opportunities for adventure seekers. Trails range from easy walks to more challenging hikes, providing options for a variety of fitness levels.

  • It's worth noting that the tourism landscape can evolve, and it's advisable to check with local tourism offices or official sources for the latest information on attractions, accommodations, and travel guidelines. Sustainable and responsible tourism practices are encouraged to preserve the natural and cultural assets of the region.
